About Me

Growing up i always enjoyed music, but it soon sparked my interest to delve deeper into the nature of acoustics and also the reproduction of sound through electronic signals.

At around 16 years old i purchased my first set of technics 1210 mk5g turntables, these kept me up for hours endlessly creating mixes and perfecting them. Playing with vinyl was such a great experience i remember, you couldn't help but purchase more each time to open up your track list selection.

Later making music was the next stop, Reason 3.0 was my introduction to the virtual production environment. I enjoyed Reasons concept, you plug cables in at the back of the rack. This concept supported my understanding of signal flow through chains of devices in audio systems.

Some quotes from my audio lecturer Broady said, "you can't polish a bad audio stream". Its very true with audio, everything in the chain has a consequence and is up to being clean up until the ADC.
